Tips for an Effective Study Plan

Make the most of your timetable with these evidence-based strategies.

Keep sessions between 45–60 minutes
Research shows focus drops sharply after 60 minutes. Shorter sessions with breaks outperform long, uninterrupted studying by a significant margin.
Vary your subjects each day
Interleaving different subjects during a study session improves long-term retention compared to studying one subject in a single long block.
Use breaks properly — don't skip them
Breaks are not wasted time. Stand up, move around, and step away from screens during every break. This resets your focus for the next session.
Always end with the review session
The review slot at the end of your timetable is the most important one. Spend it revisiting the key points from every subject you studied that day.
