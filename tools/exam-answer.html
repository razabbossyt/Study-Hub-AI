Tips for Better Exam Answers

Use these techniques alongside the generator to maximise your marks.

Always follow the Introduction–Explanation–Conclusion structure
This three-part structure is expected in almost every exam. Markers reward answers that are clearly organised — the structure shows you understand the question.
Use the key points as your revision checklist
The Key Points section is your evidence layer. In your real exam answer, each bullet should be expanded into a full sentence with supporting details or examples.
Read the generated answer, don't just copy it
Use this tool as a learning scaffold — read the answer, understand why each part is there, then close it and write your own version. That's how you actually remember it.
Match the detail level to your question type
Short "State" or "Define" questions need Concise mode. "Explain", "Describe", or "Discuss" questions need Standard or Detailed — they're worth more marks and expect more depth.
