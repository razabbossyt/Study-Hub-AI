Tips for Effective Flashcard Revision

Use these techniques alongside the flashcards for maximum retention.

Use Q:/A: format in your notes for best results
If your notes already use Q: and A: labels, the tool detects them automatically and creates highly accurate flashcards. This is especially useful for revision guides and past paper answers.
Use the "Got it / Still learning" buttons honestly
Only mark a card as "Got it" if you could recall the answer without hesitation. The progress tracker shows you exactly which cards to focus on in your next study session.
Shuffle cards after each pass to fight familiarity bias
Your brain adapts to order very quickly. Shuffling after every round forces you to recall answers without relying on position cues — making revision far more effective.
Study in short, frequent sessions rather than long ones
20 minutes of focused flashcard study three times a day outperforms one 60-minute session. Use the Pomodoro Timer alongside flashcards for optimal memory formation.
