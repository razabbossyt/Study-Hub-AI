The Pomodoro Technique

How the Pomodoro Method Works

A time-management system proven to dramatically improve focus and reduce mental fatigue.

1
Choose One Task
Pick a single subject or task to focus on. Multitasking kills productivity — commit to one thing for the session.
2
Work for 25 Minutes
Study with full concentration for 25 minutes. No phone, no social media — just deep, focused work on your chosen task.
3
Take a 5-Min Break
Step away from your desk, move around, and give your brain a rest. Do not study during the break — this is critical.
4
Repeat × 4, then Rest
After 4 full focus sessions, take a longer 15-minute break. Your brain has earned it — this resets focus for the next block.

Tips for Maximum Focus

Get the most from every Pomodoro session with these habits.

Write down your task before starting
Before pressing Start, write the specific task you'll work on. "Study biology" is vague — "Revise cell respiration pages 34–41" is actionable and focused.
Actually take the break — don't skip it
Students who skip breaks experience a 30–40% decline in focus by the third session. Your break is not optional — it's part of the system working as designed.
Start with your hardest subject first
Schedule your most difficult subject in your first Pomodoro of the day when your willpower and focus reserves are at their highest.
Use the Study Planner to block your sessions
Use Study Hub AI's Study Planner tool to schedule blocks of Pomodoros for each subject. Knowing exactly what comes next removes decision fatigue.
