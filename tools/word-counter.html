What's Included

Everything you need in one live counter

All statistics update instantly as you type — no clicks needed.

Live Word & Character Count
Words and characters (with and without spaces) update in real time with every keystroke, so your stats are always accurate.
Reading Time Estimate
Choose between slow, average, fast, and speed-reading rates. Reading time updates automatically to match your selected speed.
Top Words Analysis
See the most frequently used words in your text with a live frequency chart. Toggle the filter to hide common words like "the" and "is".
Word Goal Tracker
Set a word count target and watch a live progress bar track your progress. The bar turns green and shows a percentage when you hit your goal.
Sentence & Paragraph Count
Accurately counts sentences (by . ! ? boundaries) and paragraphs (by line breaks), giving you a complete picture of your text structure.
Copy, Download & Clear
Copy your text to clipboard, download it as a .txt file with stats included, or clear everything with a single click.

How to Use Your Word Count Effectively

Make the most of the counter for essays, assignments, and study notes.

Set a goal before you start writing
Using the word goal feature keeps you focused and prevents over-writing. For essays, aim to hit your target without exceeding it by more than 10%.
Check your Top Words for repetition
If one word appears far more often than others, you're likely overusing it. Use the Top Words panel to spot repetitive language and improve vocabulary variety.
Use reading time to estimate exam answers
Switch to "Fast" reading speed to estimate how long a marker would spend on your answer. Most exam answers should read in under 2 minutes at fast speed.
Monitor your sentence length
Divide your word count by your sentence count to get average sentence length. Aim for 15–20 words per sentence for academic writing — too long is hard to follow.
