How To Use

Summarise any text in three steps

No account needed — paste, summarise, and copy in seconds.

1
Paste Your Text
Copy any paragraph, textbook section, article, or lecture notes and paste it into the input area.
2
Choose Your Settings
Select how long you want the summary to be and what to focus on — main ideas, key facts, causes and effects, or key terms.
3
Copy or Download
Switch between Paragraph and Bullet Point views, then copy to clipboard or download as a .txt file for offline revision.

Tips for Better Summaries

Get more useful results from the summariser with these quick techniques.

Paste complete paragraphs, not fragments
The summariser works best with complete, well-formed paragraphs. Avoid pasting bullet points or heavily formatted text — plain prose gives the cleanest summaries.
Use "Key facts & data" for science subjects
For biology, chemistry, or physics texts, the Key Facts focus extracts numbers, definitions, and measurable facts — exactly what you need for exam revision.
Use Bullets for flashcard creation
After generating bullet points, copy them and paste into the Flashcard Generator to instantly create Q&A study cards from your summary.
Check the compression percentage
The compression bar shows how much shorter your summary is vs the original. Aim for 70–85% compression for revision notes — enough detail to remember the topic.
