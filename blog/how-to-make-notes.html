How to make notes that actually help you revise

Strong notes reduce revision time because they make the next study session easier than the last one. The goal is not to write everything down. The goal is to build a version of the topic that you can scan, test, and reuse quickly.

Start with a simple structure: topic heading, key ideas, examples, and likely exam questions. Use short sentences or bullets instead of long paragraphs.

  • Use one page or one section per concept.
  • Highlight only definitions, formulas, and recurring mistakes.
  • Add a short self-test question under each section.
