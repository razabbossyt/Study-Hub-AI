Core Idea

Why active recall works

Active recall means trying to produce the answer before looking at the material. That act of retrieval strengthens memory and exposes weak understanding better than passive review.

The method can be simple: close the book, answer out loud, then verify. The small struggle before checking is what makes the revision effective.

  • Turn chapter headings into questions.
  • Use blank paper to rebuild a topic from memory.
  • Check mistakes immediately, then repeat later.
