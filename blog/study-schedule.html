How to build a schedule you can actually follow

A good schedule fits real energy and real time. If the plan ignores school hours, travel, or fatigue, it will fail even if it looks organized.

Start with deadlines, then assign focused blocks to the most important subjects first. Leave space for review and catch-up so a single bad day does not break the whole week.

  • Plan weekly, then adjust daily.
  • Keep sessions specific: chapter, topic, or question set.
  • Track completion, not just intention.
