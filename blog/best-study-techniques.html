Key Methods

The highest-value techniques for exam prep

The most reliable exam methods are active recall, spaced repetition, interleaving, and timed practice. These work because they force the brain to retrieve and reorganize information instead of just seeing it again.

Build your revision around questions, not rereading. Test yourself from memory first, then check gaps, then repeat later. When possible, rotate subjects instead of spending hours on one topic only.

  • Use flashcards or blank-page recall for active retrieval.
  • Repeat difficult material on a schedule instead of cramming once.
  • Practice under time pressure before the real exam.
