Why spaced repetition is so effective

Memory fades quickly after first exposure. Spaced repetition works by revisiting information just before you are likely to forget it, which strengthens retention more efficiently than massed review.

It does not require perfect software. A simple review calendar and a set of flashcards already cover the core idea.

  • Review new material soon after learning it.
  • Increase the gap after each successful recall.
  • Short, repeated recall beats long passive rereading.
