How to use the Pomodoro technique for studying

The Pomodoro method helps you start when motivation is low. A small, fixed work block feels easier to begin and easier to repeat than a vague two-hour session.

Choose one task, work without switching tabs, then take a real break. The break matters because it protects focus over multiple rounds.

  • Plan the next session before the timer starts.
  • Keep your break short and away from heavy distraction.
  • Track how many focused rounds each subject needs.
